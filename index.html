Welcome to the Heart of Babylon

from Persia to Portland

When Ava Moradi immigrated to America in the early 1970s she wanted to share a part of her home country with her new neighbors in Oregon. She began hosting dinner parties at which she showcased some of her favorite traditional Persian recipes. Soon her friends and neighbors were asking her to cook for their own events, and offering to pay her to do so. And thus The Heart of Babylon was born. What began as a modest, one-woman endeavor, operating out of the kitchen of her home in the southeast of Portland, soon expanded into a thriving, full-service restaurant. The Heart of Babylon has been a fixture in the Sunnyside neighborhood ever since, and is still owned and operated by the Moradi family to this day. Pay us a visit and find out for yourself why the renowned Portland Monthly has dubbed the Moradis "Portland's first family of Persian Cuisine."
